Section 1 — The Core Value Proposition

Users aren't buying lessons, tools, or features. They're buying a result. The question they're asking (consciously or not): "Will this get me unstuck?"

The Promise: In 90 days, you'll go from creatively stuck and professionally anxious to clear, confident, and moving — with proof you can see and a toolkit you can use forever.

The Two Types of Value

Tangible Value
What You Can Measure
Concrete deliverables, completed artifacts, visible progress. The stuff you can point to and say "I made that" or "I did that."
Examples: Completed Blueprint, 9 tools mastered, 27 reflections captured, path confirmed, wins logged
Intangible Value
What You Can Feel
Internal shifts in confidence, clarity, and capability. The transformation that's hard to quantify but impossible to fake.
Examples: Fear doesn't stop you, decisions feel easier, creative identity restored, momentum feels natural

Why both matter: Tangible value provides proof ("Look what I accomplished"). Intangible value provides staying power ("I'm different now"). Programs that only deliver one fail — either you can't prove it worked, or the results don't last.

Section 7 — Common Objections (And Honest Answers)

"I don't have time for a 90-day program"
Each day takes 10-20 minutes. That's less than you spend scrolling. The question isn't whether you have time — it's whether you'll spend that time on transformation or distraction.
"I've tried programs before and they didn't work"
Most programs dump content and leave you alone. PYC has built-in application days, AI check-ins, and a structure that makes completion the default. 90% of users who start Day 1 finish Day 90.
"Can't I just use ChatGPT for this?"
ChatGPT doesn't remember your creative history, your blockers, your wins. It can't guide you through a proven 90-day arc. And it won't hold you accountable. PYC's Co-Pilot knows you.
"What if I fall behind?"
Life happens. You can pause and resume. The structure is firm (no skipping) but forgiving (catch up at your pace). The only way to fail is to quit completely.
"Is this just positive thinking woo-woo?"
No affirmations. No vision boards. PYC is neuroscience, psychology, and practical tools. Every concept is backed by research. Every tool is designed for the skeptical professional.
"What if I'm not sure which path (Pivot/Build/Evolve) is right?"
That's exactly what Sprint 2 is for. By Day 20, you'll have clarity. If you're unsure now, that's the point — PYC helps you decide, not just execute.
